Expert Advice

Best Pet Care Tips

Follow these expert guidelines recommended by our veterinarians to keep your furry companion healthy and happy.

1
Do not miss the de-worming schedule prescribed by your veterinarian.
2
Do not miss the vaccination time of your pet — it's critical for immunity.
3
Always go for regular health check-ups to catch problems early.
4
Always keep your pet clean, dry, and well-groomed.
5
Brush your pet's coat at least once a day to maintain hygiene.
6
Go for regular dental care — they need it as much as we do!
7
Get your pet's nails clipped, ears and anal gland cleaned regularly.
8
Consult your vet for the necessary first aid, if your pet gets injured.

Dental disease affects the teeth and structures of your pet's mouth. It begins with plaque buildup containing bacteria and food particles. Regular brushing helps prevent serious oral and systemic health problems.
Rabies is a serious, almost 100% fatal disease when contracted. It is a zoonotic disease — meaning it spreads to humans through infected animals. Vaccination is critical and often legally required.
Dogs get heartworm from infected mosquitoes. The larvae enter the bloodstream and travel to the heart, lungs, and associated blood vessels, causing serious health complications over time.
Dental disease is the most common reason for bad breath in pets. Left untreated, oral infections can cause serious problems including kidney and heart disease. Regular dental cleanings are essential.
A puppy's immune system develops slowly from 6 to 16 weeks. During this period, boosters every 4 weeks are necessary. After 6 months, a regular annual vaccination schedule is recommended.
